# 02 — Talking the Talk: Key Terminology

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Step 01 complete — you understand what a decision matrix is and why objectivity matters.

## Context (Why This Matters)

Using the right vocabulary when discussing performance with stakeholders, clients, and team members inspires confidence in your work. Misusing terms — or using them interchangeably when they mean different things — creates confusion in meetings, documents, and contracts. Precision in language reflects precision in thinking, and a team that speaks the same language avoids costly misunderstandings when building the decision matrix.

## Instructions

1. **Memorize these six core terms:**

   | Term | Definition |
   |---|---|
   | **Criterion** (or Attribute) | A quality of something that you care to measure |
   | **Metric** | A formally defined way of measuring something |
   | **Criteria Score** (or Result / Value) | The result of applying a metric to a criterion |
   | **Rubric** | A group of metrics used to measure a collective group of criteria |
   | **Decision Matrix** | A specific kind of rubric — combined scores from a group of metrics laid out in a matrix, used to help make a decision |
   | **Weight** (or Priority) | The importance of any single criterion relative to the others |

2. **Distinguish "metric" from "rubric."** A single metric measures a single criterion. A group of metrics is a rubric. In practice, people often refer to a group of metrics as "a metric" — but the precise term is **rubric**. Use the correct term in formal documents.

3. **Express weights as percentages, not arbitrary scales.** Instead of saying "this criterion has a weight of 4 out of 5," say: *"This criterion counts towards 25% of the total performance score."* Percentages make relative importance immediately clear and avoid the "percentage surprise" effect discussed later in [Step 10](10%20-%20Assigning%20Weights%20to%20Criteria.md).

## Worked Example

Apply each term to the laptop selection scenario from Step 01:

| Term | Laptop Example |
|---|---|
| **Criterion** | Battery Life |
| **Metric** | Hours of runtime under a standard workload (web browsing, video calls, document editing) |
| **Criteria Score** | Option A = 10 hours, Option B = 6 hours, Option C = N/A (cloud device, depends on thin client) |
| **Rubric** | The full set of metrics across Battery Life, Cost, Weight, CPU Performance, etc. |
| **Decision Matrix** | The spreadsheet combining all rubric scores with weights to rank Options A, B, and C |
| **Weight** | Battery Life counts for 15% of the total performance score |
